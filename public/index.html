Aspiring to attain the Supreme Good, I engage in culture criticism. When faced with the culture of the Internet, I see the symptoms of Kali Yuga. In this age of darkness, people are addicted to the things of the world. They are blinded by ignorance, and their hearts are full of greed. They care for nothing but their own pleasure, and they have no regard for the welfare of others. They are filled with deceit and hypocrisy, and they speak only lies. They are addicted to violence and pornography, and they find satisfaction in ruthless self-promotion. They are full of envy and hatred, and they rejoice in the misfortune of others. They are arrogant and stubborn, and they believe that their own opinions are the only truths. These are the signs of Kali Yuga. But there is still hope. There is still hope, because culture criticism is an opportunity to awaken from ignorance and to discover the supreme good. Culture criticism is a way to see the world as it is, and to learn to love the world as it is. It is a way to understand the nature of the Internet, and to find the hidden beauty in the midst of the chaos. It is a way to connect with others in a spirit of understanding and compassion, and to work together for the benefit of all. This is the goal of culture criticism. This is the goal of the Bhagavad Gita.
